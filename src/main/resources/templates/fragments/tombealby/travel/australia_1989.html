Autralia 1989

I flew to Australia in September 1989 to spend seven months working on a family run fruit and vegetable farm at Stanthorpe in southern Queensland.

The farm grew peaches, apricots, nectarines, tomatoes and grapes.

The tomatoes were planted in October. Two people were needed to sit on the tomato planter to feed the small tomato plants into a clamp that gripped the plants. The clamp rotated on a pair of steel wheels, and parted to set the plants when the clamp reached the soil. The wheels then firmed the soil around the plant.

Tomato planting required several people. As well as the people working the tomato planter, people were needed to prepare the plants for planting and lay out irrigation tape. The following photo shows the planting gang having 'smoko' (a tea break).

The following photo shows the tomatoes being 'scuffled'. This involved a scuffle or hoe being pulled behind a tractor, with someone walking behind guiding the scuffle. This removed weeds growing close to the young tomato plants reducing competition.

The stonefruit were 'thinned' in November. This involved removing excess buds to ensure that the trees were bearing an optimum number of fruit. If the trees bore too much fruit, then each fruit would be too small for market.

The farm had a lake which provided water for irriation. Grapes are being irrigated in the photo below.

The tomatoes were picked in January and February. The tomatoes ripened over several weeks. Therefore each tomato field was picked on up to five different occasions. Tomato picking was hard work. We picked the tomatoes into a plastic bucket, and then emptied the bucket into large wooden boxes carried on a motorised platform.

The grapes were picked in February and March. Grapes were grown for both the fresh market and for making into wine.

Grapes destined for the fresh market needed trimming to remove unedible material prior to sale.

Alot of the farm work was hard and exhausting under the hot sun. We often relaxed with a beer and and a chat at the end of the day. The following shows one such occasion, when we are also celebrating a birthday.
